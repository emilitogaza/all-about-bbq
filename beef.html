4.2Reading the animal

Notice the pattern on the diagram: the middle of the back (rib, short loin, sirloin) is steak country — lazy postural muscle, tender, cooked hot and fast. Everything at the ends and underside (chuck, brisket, plate, round, shank) worked for a living — collagen country, cooked low and slow. The flank and sirloin sit in between, which is why they produce the interesting hybrid cuts like tri-tip and flank steak: flavorful, quick, but demanding about slicing.

Grading, briefly

USDA grades measure marbling: Select (lean), Choice (solid middle), Prime (heavily marbled, restaurant-bound). For low & slow cuts like brisket, grade matters a lot — fat is your insurance policy over a 12-hour cook. Japanese Wagyu (A5 at the top) is a different scale entirely and mostly a hot-and-fast, tiny-portion luxury.

Steak doneness

Rare 49–52°C · Medium-rare 54–57°C · Medium 60–63°C · beyond that we legally cannot help you. Pull steaks ~3°C under target; they keep climbing while they rest. And they always rest.

The graduation cook

The path through beef: master tri-tip (1 hour, forgiving), then chuck roast (6 hours, brisket rehearsal), then plate short ribs (9 hours, hard to ruin), and finally the full packer brisket — the final exam of barbecue. Pass that, and there is nothing left on the animal that can intimidate you.
